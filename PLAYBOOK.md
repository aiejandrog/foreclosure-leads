# THE PLAYBOOK — every way this operation makes money, and how DEALFLOW runs it

_Distilled from the working sessions with Jose (15+ years wholesaling South Florida distress).
This is the operating manual: deal structures, numbers, scripts, roles, rules, and the tool map.
Read it once a week until it's muscle memory. Carlos reads sections 5–7 to start._

## 0. The machine in one paragraph

We are a **one-stop shop for distressed homeowners**. Whatever the owner wants — save the home,
sell it, borrow against it, or leave with dignity and cash — we have a product and a way to get
paid. The database finds them, Carlos reaches them and intakes, Alejandro structures the deal,
Jose closes or blesses it. Every step before a signature is free; every kill criterion is written
down before a dollar moves.

---

## 1. The deal ladder — what we can offer ANY owner

In order of preference, but **take what the situation gives you.** Every owner gets pitched the
rung that fits, never a one-size sermon.

1. **Equity purchase (the core flip)** — owner has real equity. Buy at a discount, assign or
   close-and-resell. The deposit from the end buyer pays the owner's share; we're only out the
   first few thousand.
2. **Stop the sale + rescue window** — $2,500 fee + ~$330 court costs to halt the auction and
   open 60–90 days. Then decide: keep, flip, or refi.
3. **Hard equity loan (owner keeps the house)** — owner has equity and wants to stay. 5-day
   funding into an LLC of their choosing. 5 points total: 2 us, 1 lender, 1–2 financing side.
   On a $300k loan that's $6k for a referral, zero ownership risk.
4. **Refi harvest (balloon mortgages coming due)** — find LLCs holding hard-money balloons,
   offer 6–7.75% vs their 10–12%, charge points. First year is break-even for them; years
   2–20 are pure savings. They bring their friends.
5. **DSCR / investment-loan hold (we keep it)** — cheap rent-based debt (5.5–7%) instead of
   10–12% hard money. Now holding makes sense: $1,400 payment vs $3,200 rent.
6. **Thin-equity dignity buy** — owner has nothing left. Buy them 60–90 days of dignity plus
   movers ($2–5k + $500–800 moving help), take the house for the rental spread or sell to a
   buy-and-hold investor. Small money, high volume, perfect training ground.
7. **Surplus / referral feed** — when a deal isn't ours, it's somebody's: Realtors pay 1–2%
   referral on deals we hand them (they close at full list in ~10 days via blast + cinematic tours).

## 2. The money, structure by structure

### 2.1 Equity purchase / assignment
- **Offer shape:** cash now, rest on move-out. _"$5k today, $45k when you hand me clean keys."_
- **Self-policing:** appliances in, pool clean, floors swept, nothing left behind. Garbage left =
  removal charged **double** out of their remainder. No police needed — the contract polices itself.
- **Funding:** assignable contract, cash-only, **zero financing contingency**, 5–10% buyer deposit.
  The **deposit pays the owner** — our exposure is the first $5k, the rest is the buyer's money.
- **Pricing:** take 3 of 4 slices. Sell at ~75% to move in 30 days instead of chasing full price
  over 90–120. **Volume > per-deal greed.** Target: 2 properties/month at $20–30k average.

### 2.2 Stop-the-sale window
- Fee: $2,500 + ~$330 court costs. Stops the auction, opens 60–90 days.
- In that window the owner chooses: keep (loan/refi), sell (assignment), or move with cash.
- The stop-sale itself is also a product — owners pay for time.

### 2.3 Hard equity loan (owner keeps)
- Owner owes $150k on a $400k property ($200k equity): 5-business-day funding into an LLC they
  name. 5 points: 2 us, 1 lender, 1–2 financing. We never hold the risk — we hold the referral.

### 2.4 Balloon refi harvest (the financing side's machine)
- **Target:** LLC-held hard-money mortgages (balloon notes, 1–2yr, 10–14%) originated ~2 years back.
- **Offer:** 6–7.75% on a 5/10/15/20-yr term, no balloon, no prepay penalty.
- **Points:** 4–5 total (1 to us). Year one is break-even for the borrower (points ≈ their old rate);
  years after are pure savings. One happy investor brings five more — they flock.
- **Why it compounds:** $2,500/month saved × 5–10 properties × 9 years, then referrals.

### 2.5 DSCR hold / portfolio track
- DSCR and straight investor loans at 5.5–6.75%, 5–10 day close (faster at ~7%).
- Rule: if rent ÷ payment works, keep it. $3,200 rent vs $1,400 payment = keep.
- **The inside structure:** contract at $250k in one LLC → sell to the keeping entity at $345k
  (arm's-length paper, appraisal supports it). Seller covers ~3% closing costs. 90% financing,
  no money down. Profit at purchase ≈ $50–70k before a tenant ever signs.
- **Splits:** start 66/33 (Jose/Alejandro); percentage grows with how much day-to-day you carry.
- **The long game:** 5 kept/yr × 5 yrs = 25 doors ≈ $1k/mo each ≈ **$25k/mo passive**, plus
  5–7%/yr appreciation, plus rent surplus crushing 30-yr loans into 7–8-yr payoffs. Exit:
  liquidate the portfolio, even at 90¢ on the dollar, and retire.

### 2.6 Thin-equity dignity buy
- The pitch to an owner with zero equity and zero options: _"In 20–30 days the sheriff puts
  everything on the street. We buy you 60–90 days of dignity, movers paid, no surprises."_
- Cost: $2–5k + $500–800 movers (one cheap company on retainer).
- Exit: rental spread, or sell to an investor who wants a tenant-ready property.
- **Why it matters:** it's how you learn to close without risking the big deals. Practice on
  $1–3k outcomes, not on $150k ones. (The father wolf: walk down quietly and eat daily.)

## 3. Financing menu — know the three lanes cold

| Lane | Cost | Speed | Use it for |
|---|---|---|---|
| Hard money | 10–12% + 2–3 pts | 2–3 days | Emergency stops only. Expensive oxygen. |
| DSCR (rent-based) | 5.5–6.5% | 5–10 days | Duplex/tri/fourplex + investment holds. The default for keeps. |
| Straight investor loan (credit-based) | 5.5–6.75% | ~5 business days | Non-homestead purchases, short-notice closes. |

The arbitrage is the whole game: hard money is what desperate people pay; DSCR/straight is what
organized people pay. We move deals from the first lane to the second and charge for the bridge.

## 4. Target selection (who to call, in what order)

1. **Fresh cases first** — late 2024 through late 2025 filings. They haven't stalled before;
   they still believe there's a way out, and they're right.
2. **Skip the 2019–2023 serial stallers** — multiple bankruptcies, rehearsed stalls. They
   don't bite; they spar.
3. **Verified equity prioritizes the day** — but never skip the small deals waiting for a whale.
   The chain-check happens BEFORE any number is quoted (records → survivor math → verdict).
4. **Homestead = attorney paper, always** (FS 501.1377): mandatory contract terms, rescission
   window, equity-purchase rules. Non-negotiable, no shortcuts.

## 5. The funnel and the roles

- **DEALFLOW (the database):** finds, verifies, ranks, refreshes. Feeds the daily worklist.
- **Carlos (ground game):** handwritten-envelope letters and door knocks with HIS number on them.
  His job is intake, not closing. His script:
  > "I take information, not your time. I bring it to the people who make the magic. If you want
  > us to put every option on the table at no charge, the only courtesy I expect is that you
  > answer when we call. If you're not interested, tell me — nobody's feelings get hurt."
- **Alejandro (structure):** pulls the chain, runs the numbers, shapes the offer, brings Jose a
  deal that's 90% built. Progression: structure → defend it on the call → close solo.
- **Jose (close / blessing):** closes, or finds the defect and sends it back. The final blessing
  until you don't need it anymore.
- **Notary:** in-family first ($100 mobile beats a stranger's $75–100 and keeps money in the group).
- **Attorney:** on contingency — 20% theirs / 10% ours where recovery work applies. E-files so we
  never chase a recording.

**Cadence:** one 1-hour meeting per week — what improved, what broke, what ships next.

## 6. The outreach engine

- **Letters:** envelope **handwritten** (2× open rate vs labeled/printed). Body printed and
  personalized with property address + auction date. Carlos's number on it.
- **Door knocks:** only when equity or timing justifies the drive; letter-first on thin deals.
- **Calls:** fresh numbers from skip-tracing, 8am–8pm only, DNC-gated by the tracker.
- **Email cadence (to build):** 3–5 touches on a schedule (day 0, 2, 4, 7) that **auto-cancel
  the instant an owner replies**. No human manages it; the reply kills the sequence.
- **Follow-ups:** the tracker owns reminders — nothing lives in a head, a notebook, or a
  WhatsApp thread.

## 8. Rules of engagement (the ones that keep you out of court and out of regret)

1. **Verify before you quote.** Chain first, number second. Blue balls are for people who skip records.
2. **FS 501.1377 paper on every homestead equity purchase.** Attorney drafts, mandatory terms, rescission window.
3. **Jose's name on every document. Alejandro's name/money/accounts on NOTHING.** Standing rule, forever.
4. **FTSA + DNC compliance** on every text/call. The opt-out ledger is the evidence system.
5. **Kill criteria are written before the first dollar:** payoff too fat, senior in its own
   foreclosure, surprise third lien, equity that won't verify.
6. **Fresh cases over stallers. Volume over greed. Deposits over our cash. Family over strangers
   — until they charge stranger prices.**
7. **No mercy isn't our product; dignity is.** Owners remember who gave them 90 clean days.

## 11. The 90-day operating rhythm

- **Daily:** 8–10 contact attempts (calls/knocks) from the worklist. Fresh cases first.
- **Weekly:** 1-hour improvement meeting. What shipped, what broke, what's next.
- **Per deal:** verify chain → structure → call Jose → close or kill by criteria. No exceptions.
- **Per month:** review the funnel math (attempts → contacts → contracts → closes). The numbers
  tell you which rung of the ladder needs work.
